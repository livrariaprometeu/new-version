Título original: Meditations

Autor: Marcus Aurelius



Written between A.D. 170 and 180, Meditations is a series of personal reflections by the Roman emperor Marcus Aurelius. Never intended for publication, the work consists of private notes in which the emperor examines virtue, self-discipline, mortality, and the importance of living according to reason and nature.

Considered one of the greatest works of Stoic philosophy, Meditations has inspired readers for centuries with its timeless teachings on resilience, duty, and inner peace in the face of life's difficulties.
